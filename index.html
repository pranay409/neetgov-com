Exam Structure

Understanding the NEET Paper Before You Touch a Book

Knowing exactly what is being tested changes how you prepare. Most students discover this too late.

Biology (360 Marks)

The highest weightage subject. Botany and Zoology together carry 90 questions at 4 marks each. Almost entirely NCERT-based, which means systematic reading matters more than reference books.

  • Botany: 45 questions
  • Zoology: 45 questions
  • Class 11 and 12 equal weight
  • Diagrams frequently tested

Chemistry (180 Marks)

Split into Physical, Inorganic, and Organic. Physical Chemistry needs calculation practice. Organic Chemistry is high yield once you master mechanisms. Inorganic is mostly memorisation with logic.

  • Physical: 12 to 14 questions
  • Inorganic: 14 to 16 questions
  • Organic: 16 to 18 questions
  • NCERT tables are exam-ready

Physics (180 Marks)

The section most students underperform in. Concepts are not the issue, problem-solving speed under exam pressure is. Regular daily practice with NCERT problems followed by previous year questions fixes this.

  • Mechanics: 8 to 10 questions
  • Electrostatics and Magnetism: 8
  • Optics and Modern Physics: 8
  • Thermodynamics: 4 to 5
